## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document analyzes key competitors in the ERP market, focusing on manufacturing ERP solutions for small-to-medium businesses. Our primary competitors are NetSuite, SAP Business One, Odoo, and QuickBooks Manufacturing. We also analyze the current state (Excel + QuickBooks) that many potential customers use today.

**Key Findings:**
- NetSuite and SAP are feature-rich but extremely expensive ($50K-$500K+ TCO)
- Odoo is affordable but requires technical expertise and lacks professional support
- QuickBooks is easy to use but not designed for manufacturing
- Market gap exists for affordable, manufacturing-focused, cloud ERP with professional UX
- NetSuite UI/UX is the gold standard - we adopt similar patterns
- Multi-tenancy and modular pricing are competitive advantages

## 3. DETAILED COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

### 3.1 NetSuite (Oracle)

**Overview:**
- Market leader in cloud ERP
- Comprehensive functionality
- Excellent UI/UX (our benchmark)
- Very expensive

**Strengths:**
- Complete, mature product
- Excellent user interface
- Strong manufacturing module
- Good reporting and dashboards
- Proven scalability
- Regular updates and improvements
- Large partner ecosystem

**Weaknesses:**
- Extremely expensive ($50K-$500K+ total cost of ownership)
- Complex implementation (3-12 months)
- Requires expensive consultants
- Overkill for many SMBs
- No local Dominican Republic support
- Customization can be expensive

**Market Position:**
- Mid-market to enterprise
- 37,000+ customers globally
- Strong in North America
- Growing in LATAM but expensive for region

**Pricing Model:**
- License: $999-$1,999/month base
- Additional users: $99-$199/user/month
- Implementation: $50K-$500K
- Annual license increases

**Our Competitive Strategy Against NetSuite:**
- Price: 1/10th the cost
- Implementation: 10x faster
- Local support in Dominican Republic
- Same quality UI/UX
- Simpler, focused on manufacturing SMBs
- Position as "NetSuite for companies that can't afford NetSuite"

---

### 3.2 SAP Business One

**Overview:**
- SAP's SMB ERP solution
- Strong in manufacturing
- Enterprise credibility
- Expensive and complex

**Strengths:**
- SAP brand credibility
- Comprehensive manufacturing features
- Strong in discrete manufacturing
- Good localization options
- Mature product
- Large partner network

**Weaknesses:**
- Expensive ($1,500+/month + implementation)
- Requires SAP-certified consultants
- Complex to implement and maintain
- On-premise or hosted (not true SaaS)
- Dated UI compared to modern standards
- High total cost of ownership

**Market Position:**
- 70,000+ customers globally
- Strong in manufacturing sector
- Traditional on-premise, moving to cloud slowly

**Pricing Model:**
- Professional license: $1,500-$3,000/user/month
- Implementation: $30K-$200K
- Annual maintenance: 20% of license cost
- Requires certified partners

**Our Competitive Strategy Against SAP:**
- Modern cloud architecture vs hybrid
- Superior UI/UX
- 10x lower price point
- Faster implementation
- No consultant dependency
- Target companies evaluating SAP but finding it too expensive/complex

---

### 3.3 Odoo

**Overview:**
- Open-source ERP
- Modular architecture
- Affordable pricing
- Requires technical expertise

**Strengths:**
- Open source (free community edition)
- Very modular (pick what you need)
- Large app marketplace
- Affordable enterprise edition
- Modern technology stack (Python)
- Active developer community

**Weaknesses:**
- Requires technical expertise to configure
- Community edition lacks support
- Quality varies between modules
- Customization can break upgrades
- UI/UX is functional but not polished
- Implementation requires Odoo partners for most companies
- Manufacturing module less mature than competitors

**Market Position:**
- 7 million+ users (mostly community edition)
- Strong in Europe, growing globally
- Popular with tech-savvy SMBs

**Pricing Model:**
- Community: Free (but no support)
- Enterprise: $200/month for first user + $40/user/month
- Apps: $10-$100/month each
- Implementation: $10K-$50K with partner

**Our Competitive Strategy Against Odoo:**
- Better out-of-box manufacturing functionality
- Superior UI/UX (NetSuite-inspired vs functional)
- Professional support included
- No technical expertise required
- More polished, cohesive product
- Target companies frustrated with Odoo complexity
- Positioned as "enterprise-grade alternative to Odoo"

---

### 3.4 QuickBooks Online + Manufacturing Add-ons

**Overview:**
- Popular accounting software
- Manufacturing via third-party add-ons
- Easy to use
- Not purpose-built for manufacturing

**Strengths:**
- Well-known brand
- Easy to use
- Affordable
- Good accounting features
- Large ecosystem of add-ons
- Fast to get started

**Weaknesses:**
- Not designed for manufacturing
- Manufacturing features require multiple add-ons
- Fragmented (no single integrated solution)
- Limited BOM capabilities
- No real MRP
- No shop floor control
- Weak inventory management for manufacturing
- US-focused (DGII compliance requires customization)

**Market Position:**
- 7 million+ customers globally
- Dominant in micro and small businesses
- Weak in manufacturing sector

**Pricing Model:**
- QuickBooks: $75/month
- Manufacturing add-ons: $50-$200/month additional
- Total: $125-$275/month but fragmented

**Our Competitive Strategy Against QuickBooks:**
- Purpose-built for manufacturing
- Integrated (no add-ons needed)
- Better inventory management
- Full MRP and production planning
- Similar price point but complete solution
- Target companies outgrowing QuickBooks
- Positioned as "the manufacturing ERP for QuickBooks users"

---

### 3.5 Excel + QuickBooks (Current State)

**Overview:**
- Most common current state for small manufacturers
- Excel for MRP, production, inventory
- QuickBooks for accounting
- Manual integration between systems

**Strengths:**
- Familiar tools
- Flexible (Excel can do anything)
- Low upfront cost
- No training required
- Immediate start

**Weaknesses:**
- Manual data entry (high labor cost)
- Error-prone (formula errors, version control)
- No real-time visibility
- Doesn't scale
- No audit trail
- No multi-user concurrency
- Manual reconciliation between systems
- Formula breakage common
- Compliance challenges

**Market Position:**
- Estimated 60-80% of small manufacturers
- Default state for companies under 20 employees

**Total Cost:**
- QuickBooks: $75/month
- Excel: Included in Office 365
- Labor cost: $5K-$20K/year (manual entry, reconciliation)
- True cost: Much higher than appears

**Our Competitive Strategy Against Status Quo:**
- Demonstrate time savings (ROI calculator)
- Show error reduction
- Emphasize real-time visibility
- Prove scalability
- Position as "graduate from Excel without paying enterprise prices"
- Free trial to reduce switching friction
- Easy data migration from Excel

## 4. FEATURE COMPARISON DETAILS

### 4.1 Manufacturing Capabilities

**Bill of Materials (BOM):**
- Sunset ERP: Multi-level, substitutions, co-products, versions
- NetSuite: Excellent (multi-level, revisions, effectivity dates)
- SAP B1: Excellent (multi-level, phantoms, variants)
- Odoo: Good (multi-level, basic features)
- QuickBooks: Limited (single-level only)

**Production Planning:**
- Sunset ERP: MRP, capacity planning, scheduling
- NetSuite: Advanced (MRP, APS, demand planning)
- SAP B1: Strong (MRP, capacity planning)
- Odoo: Basic (MRP only)
- QuickBooks: None

**Shop Floor Control:**
- Sunset ERP: Work order tracking, time reporting, quality checks
- NetSuite: Comprehensive (mobile, barcode, real-time)
- SAP B1: Good (work orders, confirmations)
- Odoo: Basic (work order status)
- QuickBooks: None

**Costing:**
- Sunset ERP: Standard, actual, variance analysis
- NetSuite: Advanced (standard, average, FIFO, lot-based)
- SAP B1: Comprehensive (multiple methods, multilevel)
- Odoo: Basic (standard costing)
- QuickBooks: Limited (average cost only)

### 4.2 User Experience

**UI Quality:**
- Sunset ERP: Excellent (NetSuite-inspired)
- NetSuite: Excellent (industry leader)
- SAP B1: Good (improving with HANA)
- Odoo: Fair (functional but not polished)
- QuickBooks: Good (simple, clean)

**Learning Curve:**
- Sunset ERP: Moderate (2-3 days for basic users)
- NetSuite: Steep (1-2 weeks formal training)
- SAP B1: Steep (1 week+ training required)
- Odoo: Moderate (varies by module)
- QuickBooks: Easy (same day)

**Mobile Access:**
- Sunset ERP: Planned (v2.0)
- NetSuite: Yes (native apps)
- SAP B1: Yes (mobile app)
- Odoo: Yes (responsive web)
- QuickBooks: Yes (native app)

## 5. PRICING COMPARISON

### 5.1 Total Cost of Ownership (3 Years, 10 Users)

**Sunset ERP:**
- Subscription: $199/mo x 36 months = $7,164
- Implementation: $2,000
- Training: $500
- Total 3-year TCO: $9,664
- **Cost per user per year: $322**

**NetSuite:**
- Subscription: $999 base + ($99 x 10 users) x 36 = $71,604
- Implementation: $100,000
- Training: $10,000
- Total 3-year TCO: $181,604
- **Cost per user per year: $6,053**

**SAP Business One:**
- Subscription: $1,500/user/mo x 10 x 36 = $540,000
- Implementation: $75,000
- Training: $15,000
- Annual maintenance: $0 (included)
- Total 3-year TCO: $630,000
- **Cost per user per year: $21,000**

**Odoo Enterprise:**
- Subscription: $200 + ($40 x 9) x 36 = $20,160
- Apps: $50/mo x 36 = $1,800
- Implementation: $25,000
- Training: $3,000
- Total 3-year TCO: $49,960
- **Cost per user per year: $1,665**

**QuickBooks + Add-ons:**
- QuickBooks: $75/mo x 36 = $2,700
- Manufacturing add-ons: $150/mo x 36 = $5,400
- Implementation: $1,000
- Training: $500
- Total 3-year TCO: $9,600
- **Cost per user per year: $320**

**Excel + QuickBooks (Current State):**
- QuickBooks: $75/mo x 36 = $2,700
- Excel: $0 (included in Office)
- Labor cost: $15K/year x 3 = $45,000
- Total 3-year TCO: $47,700
- **Cost per user per year: $1,590**

**Analysis:**
- We are price-competitive with QuickBooks
- We are 18x cheaper than NetSuite
- We are 65x cheaper than SAP
- We are 5x cheaper than current Excel state (when labor included)
- We are 5x cheaper than Odoo

## 10. COMPETITIVE THREATS

### 10.1 Existing Competitor Actions

**Threat: NetSuite lowers prices**
- Probability: Low
- Impact: Medium
- Mitigation: Focus on ease of use, local support, faster implementation

**Threat: Odoo improves UX significantly**
- Probability: Medium
- Impact: Medium
- Mitigation: Stay ahead on manufacturing features, support quality

**Threat: New cloud ERP entrant**
- Probability: Medium
- Impact: Medium
- Mitigation: First-mover advantage, customer lock-in, continuous improvement

### 10.2 Market Threats

**Threat: Market prefers on-premise over cloud**
- Probability: Low (trend is cloud)
- Impact: High
- Mitigation: Emphasize security, uptime, cost savings

**Threat: Customers unwilling to trust startup**
- Probability: Medium
- Impact: High
- Mitigation: Free trial, pilot customers, transparent roadmap, strong SLA
